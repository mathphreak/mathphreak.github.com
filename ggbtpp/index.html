Two-Point Perspective Boxes

This is two point perspective. Drag around points A, B, C, and D, along with VP_1 and VP_2 if you'd like.

If you want to construct your own shapes:

  • Open the .ggb file.
  • Create three points: the upper corner of the box (behaves like A) and your two vanishing points (VP_1 and VP_2 above).
  • Use the Part A custom tool. NB: This only creates three lines. If I didn't do this, then you wouldn't be able to resize the box.
  • Use the Part B custom tool. The horizontal lines were necessary for the conditional visibility that's only available above...if you're really clever, you can recreate the conditional visibility stuff. It didn't work for custom tool output.
  • Drag around your three points created as you selected stuff at the beginning of part B (B, C, and D above). This makes GeoGebra realize that the points aren't in the same place as it assumed they would be.
Sorry about the resizing as you drag the top corner. It works when you're dealing with points not created by a custom tool, but custom tools somehow behave differently.
